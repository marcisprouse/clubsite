    1.         NAME.     The name of the corporation shall be COYOTE LAKES RECREATION CLUB INC., hereafter referred to as "Club."
    2.         ADDRESS.     The principal office of the corporation shall be in Coyote Lakes Recreation Club, 18860 N. Coyote Lakes Parkway, Surprise, Arizona 85374. The mailing address shall be prescribed by the Board of Directors.
    3.         PURPOSE.     The purpose for which the Club was formed is to acquire real property known as Coyote Lakes Community Center and to maintain and operate a recreation club in Surprise, Arizona for the benefit of its members and guests.
    4.         SEAL.     The corporate seal be circular in form and shall have inscribed thereon in the outer circle, the words "COYOTE LAKES RECREATION CLUB, INC." and in the inner circle the words "CORPORATE SEAL, 2001 ".


    1.         MEMBERSHIP. Membership shall be limited to individuals, married couples and corporations who are owners of dwellings in COYOTE LAKES and who are approved by the Board of Directors as hereinafter provided. A husband and wife shall share a single membership. COYOTE LAKES shall be defined as the residential property within the boundaries of Coyote Lakes, Surprise, Arizona 85374.
    2.         CHARTER MEMBERSHIPS. Charter memberships will be offered to eligible individuals, married couples, and corporations who qualify under paragraph 1, above, for a membership fee of $1000.00 until 6:00 p.m. on October 1, 2001. Other memberships may be sold subsequently for such amounts and in such number as the Board of Directors shall determine from time to time. Memberships shall be evidenced by a Certificate of membership.
    3.         APPLICATION. Prospective members shall submit an application for membership on such forms as shall be approved from time to time by the Board of Directors. In addition to providing names, addresses, and telephone numbers (winter and summer) and such other information as the Board of Directors shall deem pertinent, such application shall contain an acknowledgement that the prospective member has received and read a copy of the current Bylaws and Rules and Regulations governing membership. The Membership Committee shall promptly review all applications and recommend acceptance or rejection to the Board of Directors. A negative vote of 3 or more Directors shall constitute rejection by the Board of Directors. The proceedings of the Board of Directors in which a membership application is rejected shall be kept confidential.
    4.         MEMBERSHIP RESALES AND TRANSFERS.
      1. DWELLINGS OWNERSHIP. Memberships will be sold to owners of dwellings in COYOTE LAKES only. A membership must be surrendered to the Club when the member sells his or her dwelling in COYOTE LAKES and the membership shall be resold by the Club as hereinafter provided.
      2. RESIGNATION. A membership may also be surrendered to the club by a member desiring to resign. The member must pay annual dues until the membership is sold. The member retains all rights of use of the club until the sale is completed. Any dues or other obligations to the club that are unpaid will be deducted from the refund of the membership.
      3. RESALE BY CLUB. Except as hereinafter provided with respect to sale of membership in conjunction with the sale of the dwelling with which it is associated, memberships of members who become ineligible and resigning members shall be resold by the Club in the order surrendered, with net proceeds of the sale paid to the surrendering member as hereinafter provided.
      4. WAITING LISTS. In the event there is more than one membership waiting to be resold, a seller's waiting list shall be established. If the Club has no memberships available, a buyer's waiting list will be established, and as a membership becomes available it will be offered by the Club to the prospective member at the top of the buyer's waiting list. Prospective members shall be placed on the waiting list in the order in which they are approved by the Board of Directors as provided in Article II, subparagraph 1. If the prospective buyer fails to purchase a membership when offered, that buyer shall be removed from the list. The buyer's waiting list shall be reviewed at least once a year by the Membership Committee to insure member candidates desire to remain on the list. Members are required to surrender their membership certificate immediately upon asking to be place on the for sale list. Members may continue to participate in the Club activities until dues expire or membership is sold, at which time their key must be surrendered.
      5. SALE WITH DWELLING. Notwithstanding the foregoing to the contrary, if a member is selling his or her dwelling in COYOTE LAKES, and the purchaser of that member's dwelling desires to purchase the selling member's membership with fifth teen (15) days of close of escrow on the dwelling.
      6. RESALE PRICE. The resale price of a membership on the resale list shall be the current price at the time ofresale as determined by the BOD. The selling member shall receive the original fee paid, less unpaid dues or other obligations to the club.
      7. EXCEPTIONS. In the event a membership is owned by a married couple and one of the spouses die, if the surviving spouse continues to own the dwelling and the membership, the membership shall be surrendered and reissued in the name of the surviving spouse without a transfer fee. Similarly, if the dwelling and the membership passed on the death of a member to an heir, the membership shall be surrendered and reissued to such heir without a transfer fee.
        1.         A member can sell their membership directly to another COYOTE LAKES resident for any price, bypassing the Club for sale list, as long as the seller pays the difference between the current sale price and the seller's original membership cost. Then their membership certificate can be submitted for reissue when the current transfer fee is paid.


    1.         NAME.    DUES. Annual membership dues shall be recommended by the Finance Committee and submitted for approval or modification by the Board of Directors at the annual meeting or any special meeting of the Board of Directors and shall be due and payable in advance by the members of the date or dates established by the Board of Directors.
      1. A member who sells his or her membership with their dwelling may transfer any unused dues to the buyer along with their membership and submit their certificate to the Club for reissue to the buyer with payment of the transfer fee. A new member may be allowed to pay pro-rated dues. There shall be no refund of unused dues.
    2.         DELINQUENCY. In the event that membership dues or any other obligations to the Club are not paid on or before the assigned due date, the member shall be notified in writing of the delinquency and all Club privileges shall be suspended.
    3.         REINSTATEMENT. A suspended member shall have thirty (30) days from the date of suspension in which to discharge the indebtedness in full and have membership privileges reinstated. Failure to obtain reinstatement within said period shall result in automatic expulsion.


    1. CLUB FACILITIES. Use of the Club facilities and participation in its recreational activates shall be limited to members, their dependents and guest, and Member Designees and their dependents and guest. For purposes of these Bylaws, "Member Designee" is defined as an individual or married couple who rents or leases the dwelling within COYOTE LAKES which is associated with the membership, and in the case of a corporate membership, an individual or married couple residing in the dwelling within COYOTE LAKES which is associated with the corporation membership. Any member designee shall be registered with the Club in writing by completing and submitting forms provided by the Club which shall be signed by the owner of the membership before exercising the rights provided under this paragraph. Member designees may be revoked or changed from time to time by the owner of the membership. The owner of the membership shall exercise no rights while a member designee is in effect, except the right to vote. The Board of Directors shall establish policies and procedures for identifying persons described in this paragraph (members, member designees, dependents, and guests) and place limitations on use by dependents and guest. Conduct of member designees, dependents and guest shall conform to the conduct required of members. Club members shall be responsible for the conduct of all member designees, dependents and guest. No dependent or guest shall enjoy greater privileges than the host member is entitled to. The Entertainment Committee shall execute procedures for the members and/or guest attendance for all Social Activities of the Club; see Article 9, Section 2 D, in the First amendment dated February 4, 2002.
    2. ANNUAL MEETING OF MEMBERS. The annual meeting of the members shall be held on any Monday designated from time to time by the Board of Directors during the period between the third Monday in November and the second Monday in December. At the annual meeting, members shall elect three (3) directors as hereinafter provided in A1iicle IV and transact such other business as is properly brought before the meeting. Written notice of the annual meeting stating the place, date and hour of the meeting shall be given to each neither member of record not less than ten (10) or more than fifty (50) days before the date of such meeting.
    3. SPECIAL MEETING OF MEMBERS. Special meetings of members may be called by the President upon his or her decision, at the request in writing three (3) members of the Board of Directors, or at the request in writing of ten ( 10) percent of the members for any purpose unless otherwise proscribed by statue or the Articles of Incorporation.
    4. NOTICE OF MEETINGS. Written notice of annual or special meeting stating the place, date and hour of the meeting, the purpose and agenda including a brief description of each agenda item shall be given to each member ofrecord not less than fourteen (14) days before the date of the meeting. Items of business not included on the agenda may be presented, considered and acted upon at annual meetings. Special meetings (refer to Article IV, paragraph 3). Items to be place on the agenda for special meetings shall be submitted at least seven (7) days prior to meeting date in writing to the Board of Directors. Business transacted at any special meeting shall be limited to the purpose stated in the notice.
    5. PLACE OF MEETINGS. The place of meetings shall be fixed by the Board of Directors and unless otherwise specified in the notice, all meetings of membership shall be held at the Clubhouse.
    6. VOTING. Members in good standing shall be eligible to attend meetings of the Club. Each membership certificate holder shall have one vote at annual or special meetings of the membership. One third (1/3) of the members of the Club present in person or represented by proxy shall constitute a quorum at all meetings of the members. When a quorum is present at any meeting, the vote of a majority of those present, whether in person or by proxy shall decide any question brought before such meeting, unless otherwise specified by statue, the Articles of Incorporation, or these Bylaws. Member designees as defined in paragraph 1 of this Article IV shall not have the right to vote except by duly executed proxy from the membership certificate holder. Only one vote may be cast for each membership, whether owned by an individual, married couple, or corporation. If a membership is held by a corporation, the president of a corporate member shall designate in writing to the Board of Directors on individual who shall have the right to vote. This designation may be changed from time to time at least twenty four (24) hours prior to time for commencement as contained in the notice of any regular or special meeting of the membership at which it is to be given effect.
    7. PROXY. The Board of Directors shall provide a form of proxy to be used by a member in good standing to designate any other member or designee as defined in paragraph 1 of Article IV, to vote on his or her behave on all business presented at an annual or special meeting. The proxy must be signed by the member and include the member's membership identification number and must be presented to the Secretary at least twenty four (24) hours prior the time for commencement of the meeting as contained in the notice.
    8. CONDUCT OF MEETINGS. The President or in his or her the Vice President, Secretary, or the Treasurer, or a chairman elected from the members present shall in order preside over either an annual or special meeting. The Secretary shall function as secretary at all meetings except when he or she is presiding or absent, in which case a secretary shall be appointed by the presiding officer. All meetings of the members shall be conducted in accordance with Roberts Rules of Order.


    1. MEMBER EXPULSION. The Board of Directors may expel any member from the Club for conduct significantly injurious to the Club by a two thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors present at a properly called meeting of the Board of Directors.
      1. No member shall be so expelled until given ten (10) days notice in writing along with a written copy of the charges upon which the proposed action is taken.
      2. Upon written request such member will be afforded a proper hearing before action is taken by the Board of Directors.
      3. The Board of Directors shall have full authority to decide what conduct will be considered to institute charges and their method of investigation and procedure shall be as the Board of Directors itself shall determine and the decision of the Board of Directors shall be final.
      4. Expulsion shall in no way release the member of indebtedness to the Club, if any regarding expulsion for failure to pay dues.
      5. The membership of an expelled member shall be tendered to the Club immediately upon final determination of expulsion by the Board of Directors. Such tender shall be treated as provided in paragraph 4, subparagraph D, Article II for memberships of resigning members.


    1.         BOARD OF DIRECTORS.
      1. The business of the corporation shall be managed by the Board of Directors, subject to the Bylaws of the corporation and as such mandates may be expressed at the regular annual meeting or special meeting of the members.
      2. The initial Directors named in the Articles oflncorporation shall serve as an interim Board of Directors until the first annual meeting of the members in December of 2002. At that meeting, the members shall elect nine (9) Directors, three whom shall service for a term of one year, three of whom will serve for a term of two year, and three of whom shall serve for a term of three years. Thereafter the Board of Directors shall consist of nine (9) members who shall be persons who have been Club members for at least one (1) year. In addition to the elected members, the immediate past President shall be a non-voting member unless elected as one of the nine (9) voting members.
        1. The term of office shall begin at the close of the annual meeting at which they are elected.
        2. A director shall serve no more than two full consecutive terms. Husband and wife shall not serve on the Board of Directors at the same time, although both shall be eligible to serve at different times.
      3. VACANCIES O THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS. The Board of Directors may fill any vacancies on the Board by appointment until the next annual meeting or special meeting of the members.
      4. NOMINATIONS FOR THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS.
        1. The president shall instruct the Secretary or appoint one ( 1) regular member to be the Chair of the Nominating Committee not less than thirty (30) days prior to the annual meeting. The chair shall enlist the assistance of two (2) other regular members to complete the nominating committee.
        2. The Committee shall nominate at least one (I) regular member for each Director to be elected at the annual meeting, and shall submit its roster of nominees, including the term of office for each should an unexpired term that needs to be filled, to the Board of Directors prior to the annual meeting.
        3. The list of nominees selected by the nominating committee shall be made available to the membership at least fourteen (14) days before the annual meeting by one of the following US mail, e-mail, web site, text, or any other electronic communications, and/or distributed at club functions, including posting on the Clubhouse bulletin board.
        4. Any ten (I 0) regular members may nominate a regular member for election as a Director, if such written nomination over their signature includes a written statement from the nominee signifying his or her willingness to serve, and it is delivered to the Club secretary no later than twenty (20) days prior to the annual meeting. If there are unexpired terms, the nomination petition must indicate the term of office being sought.
        5. The names of all nominees shall be posted on the Clubhouse bulletin board at least two (2) weeks prior to the annual meeting. Also, the roster of all nominees shall be forwarded to the membership with the notice of the annual meeting by any method in paragraph 3. All nominees shall be regular members, having had such membership for one (1) or more years and shall be in good standing when nominated. A nominee for the Board of Directors shall not be the spouse of a Director whose term has not expired.
        6. The Directors to be elected at the annual meeting or at a special meeting noticed for this purpose shall be chosen by ballot. After the names have been posted, the secretary shall cause the names of all properly nominated candidates to be printed alphabetically on a suitable supply of ballots with a notice to the membership by any method in paragraph 3 to vote for " _ "number of candidates. If there are unexpired terms, the ballot shall indicate the nominees and their respective terms of office.
        7. Prior to any meeting the President shall appoint a sergeant at arms, who shall select three (3) assistants from the membership and they shall act as tellers at the meeting. These members shall be charged with the responsibility of qualifying members, maintaining order, pass out and collect ballots and to count the ballots at any election, validate the vote count and submit to the President.
      5. ELECTION OF BOARD OF DIRECTORS.
        1. Election of three (3) directors and directors for any position vacated during the year shall be held at the annual meeting.
        2. Voting shall be by ballot except when only one nominee for a position has been presented, the vote may then be by voice.
        3. Two (2) members shall be designated as judges and two (2) members shall be designated as tellers for the election. Board members shall not be eligible to serve as judges or tellers.
        4. A majority of those present and voting shall elect a Board member. Each member shall have as many votes as there are directors to be elected: provided, however, that a member may cast only one (1) vote for each candidate.
        5. A ballot listing the candidates and number of directors to be elected and an explanation of the number of votes each membership certificate holder may cast shall be mailed by regular mail to each membership holder fourteen ( 14) days prior to the election date. The ballot shall explain that a membership certificate holder may vote by checking the correct number of votes, or less, on the ballot and return by regular mail not later than seven (7) days prior to the election date, or the membership certificate holder may assign his or her vote by proxy to a qualified membership certificate holder to be cast in person at the election meeting, or the membership certificate holder may cast his or her ballot in person at the election on the election date. All proxy ballots must have the membership number of the voting member on the ballot in order to check the validity of the vote.
      6. MEETINGS OF THE BOARD OF DIRECTORS.
        1. REGULAR MEETINGS.
          1. The Board of Directors shall meet quarterly at a time established by the Board of Directors and posted on the Club bulletin board.
          2. A regular meeting of the Board of Directors, newly elected and existing shall be held without any other notice than this Bylaw immediately after, and at the same place at the annual meeting of members for the purpose of electing directors.
          3. The minutes of a regular meeting of the Board of Directors shall be posted on the bulletin board within ten (10) days following the meeting.
        2. SPECIAL MEETINGS.
          1. A special meeting of the Board of Directors may be called by the President or at the request of any two (2) Directors.
          2. Notice of a special meeting, together with items of business to be acted upon, shall be given by personal notice (including telephone, e-mail) or mail to each Director at his or her address of record. If the notice is other than by mail, it shall be given not less than 48 hours prior to the meeting. If such notice is mailed, seven (7) days mail time shall be allowed.
          3. Any Director may waive notice of any meeting if aware of it.
          4. Attendance of a Director at a meeting shall constitute a waiver of notice of such meeting.
        3. PLACE OF MEETINGS. All meetings of the Board of Directors shall be held at the Clubhouse, unless requested otherwise by the person or persons calling the meeting.
        4. QUORUM. A majority of the Directors, i.e., five (5) shall be constitute a Quorum for the transaction of business at any meeting of the Board of Directors, except where these Bylaws specify otherwise. If any Director is unable to physically attend, he or she may participate and vote by telephone, e-mail, facsimile transmission or letter provided that such participation is timely provided under the circumstances.
        5. RULES OF ORDER. The Board of Directors shall establish it own rules, order of business and method of procedure, except as these Bylaws specify otherwise.
          1. Any Board of Directors member is absent three (3) consecutive regular meetings without valid reason shall be deemed to have resigned and vacated that office.
      7. DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES.
        1. he Board of Directors shall prescribe such rules and regulations as necessary and proper for the conduct of the affairs of Club.
        2. The Board of Directors shall constitute the ruling and governing power of the Club for the above purpose as well as in all matters affecting the operation, development, and maintenance of the physical plant and the protection and welfare of the members.
        3. The Board of Directors shall authorize or ratify all expenditures.
          1. All proposals for expenditures for capital additions, replacements, and major repairs shall be submitted for preliminary approval to the Board of Directors.
          2. Proposal exceeding one hundred ($100.00) dollars shall be accompanied by detailed plans and or specifications.
          3. Upon preliminary approval by the Board of Directors, the proposed project shall, when, practical, be submitted for competitive bidding.
          4. Provide funds are available and inappropriate, the net working capital of the Club is not and will not be reduced below five thousand dollars ($5,000), and the Board of Directors may then approve formally awarding a contract the lowest acceptable bidder.
          5. The Contractor shall provide, if applicable, sufficient bond insuring performance and payment of all material and subcontractors.
          6. In the event a proposed project exceeds ten thousand dollars ($10,000) in costs, the Board of Directors must, in addition, first submit the program to the members in writing and obtain approval by a majority of the members in attendance at a duly called regular or special meeting, before formally awarding the contract, unless the expenditure is for an emergency replacement.
            1. The Board of Directors shall have the power to levy dues and determine the methods of payment not otherwise stipulated by these Bylaws.
          7. The dues structure shall be based on membership structure of the Club, and the Board of Directors shall have the authority to establish new or additional membership structure in order to prepare a viable budget.
          8. Special assessments shall not be levied without approval of a majority of members present at a duly called meeting, between October 15th and the following April 15th.
            1. The Board of Directors may authorize any officer or officer's agents or agents to enter into contract or execute and deliver any instrument in the name of and on behalf of the Club and such authority may be general or confined to specific instance, except as limited by the Bylaws, Section 6, ARTICLE VI.
            2. Loans shall not be contracted on behalf of the Club and no evidence of indebtedness shall be issued in the name of the Club, signed by such officer or officer agents or agents of the Club, except as shall be determined by the approval of a majority of membership holders present at duly called meetings, at which a quorum is present
            3. The Board of Directors shall not expend Club funds for any donation, contributions, bonuses gifts, gratuities, or loans to any employee or other individuals or corporations.


    1. The Executive Officers of the Club shall be the President, Vice-President, Treasurer, and Secretary. All such officers shall be elected annually by the Board of Directors from its members at its organizational meeting promptly after the annual meeting of the membership. They shall hold office until their respective successor is duly elected and qualified unless they resign or are removed for cause form such office by the Board of Directors. Also, the Board of Directors may designate or appoint an Assistant Secretary and an Assistant Treasurer, neither of whom needs to be a member of the Board of Directors. No member of the Board of Directors shall be eligible for election as President or Vice-President until he or she has served on the Board of Directors for at least one ( 1) full elected period, annual meeting to annual meeting, nor shall any Director hold more than one (1) Executive Office at the same time.
      1. PRESIDENT. The President shall be the chief executive officer of the Club; shall sign and execute all authorized bonds, contracts, and other obligations in the name of the Club, attested to by the Secretary; shall direct the employment or dismissal of all employees at the Club with the approval of the Board of Directors for his or her actions and shall have no authority to bind the Club except as authorized by the Board of Directors, shall not succeed himself or herself in office more than once.
      2. VICE-PRESIDENT. In the absence or disability of the President, the duties of the office shall be performed by the Vice President.
      3. TREASURER. The Treasurer shall have custody of all funds and securities of the Club and official records, documents and ledgers and accounts involving the financial business of the Club, shall be bonded in an amount as designated by the Board of Directors. The accounts and finances of the Club shall be audited or reviewed by CPA's as directed by the Board of Directors and shall be conducted and authorized by the Board of Directors. Review shall be conducted annually except in the year of an audit when no review shall be conducted. It is recommended that the maximum interval between audits should be three (3) years.
      4. SECRETARY. The Secretary shall be responsible for the minutes of all meetings of the Board of Directors and of all meetings of the membership, shall attend to and give the serving of all notices of the Club, shall act as agent and authenticate for the Club of all membership certificates and shall affix the Seal of the Club there to, shall be responsible for the maintenance of the CERTIFICATES OF MEMBERSHIP records, membership and waiting list, and for the Validation of these lists, shall be responsible of such other administrative duties as assigned by the President.
      5. ASSIST ANT TREASURER. In the absence or disability of the Treasurer, the Assistant Treasure shall perform such duties specifically assigned by the Board of Directors to assist with Treasurer during his or her absence. The Assistant Treasure shall not have Board Member voting rights unless he or she is a Board Member.
      6. ASSISTANT SECRETARY. In the absence or disability of the Secretary shall perform such duties specifically assigned by the Board of Directors to assist the Secretary during his or her absence. The Assistant Secretary shall not have Board of Director voting rights unless he or she is a Board Member.
    2. REMOVAL OF OFFICERS. Any officer elected by or appointed with the approval of the Board of Directors may be removed by the Board of Directors whenever, in its judgment, the best interests of the Club will be served.
    3. VACANCIES IN OFFICE. Vacancy in any office may be filled by the Board of Directors for the unexpired portion of the term.
    4. BONDING OF OFFICERS. Bonds shall be required by the Board of Directors in such amounts as they shall fix, from time to time, for such officers, agents, and employees as they shall deem advisable. Costs of such bonds shall be borne by the Club. Directors and officers liability insurance shall be purchased in an amount to be determined by the Board of Directors, cost to be borne by the Club.


    1. Persons using the Club facilities shall comply with the Club Rules and Regulations approved by the Board of Directors and posted on the Club bulletin board
    2. It is the policy of the Club not to tolerate sexual, verbal, or physical conduct by any member or any guest to any other member, guest, or employee.
    3. All complaints about the conduct of any officer, member, or employee of the Club must be in writing, signed by the complainant, and addressed to the Secretary of the Club. The Club shall make every effort to resolve the complaint promptly and effectively. The complainant shall be informed of the action taken on the complaint.
    4. Any regular member or other type of member who is guilty of conduct unbecoming a member in his other relationship with other members, dependents, guest, or board members, who shall inflict physical damage to the Club facilities, or who may intentionally violate these Bylaws, or the posted Rules and Regulations, may be suspended or expelled from the Club by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full Board of Directors. Also, a member may be expelled for the misconduct of a dependent. No such action shall be taken with respect to a member until that member has been afforded an opportunity to be heard in his or her behalf defense before the Board of Directors. In case of expulsion, a member's dues and privileges shall be terminated as of the effective date of expulsion by the Board of Directors. Disposition of the Certificate of Membership of an expelled regular member shall be in accordance with the provisions in Article II.


    1. PROCEDURE FOR SELECTION. The committees listed in paragraph 2 of this Article shall be the Standing Committees of the Club. Their function shall be to assist the Board of Directors in the operation of the Club within the functional responsibilities of each committee. The President shall as soon as possible after assuming office and upon approval of the Board of Directors, appoint the committee chairs to the Standing Committees. All committee chairs shall select their committee members from the general membership. Standing Committees shall have a chair and at least two (2) members. Each committee chair shall submit the names of his or her committee members to the Board of Directors. The Board of Directors may at any time change or remove any personnel of any committee and fill any vacancy existing on the committees.
    2. TITLES AND FUNCTIONS OF STANDING COMMITTEES. The committees listed in this Article shall be the standing committees of the Club. The functions, activities, and actions of these committees are governed by the requirements contained in section 3 of this Article.
      1. HOUSE COMMITTEE. Monitor the daily upkeep of the Clubhouse, review and revise the rules as needed and monitor the enforcement of such rules and regulations to the use of facilities and functions with the Entertainment Committee.
      2. FIANACE AND BUDGET COMMITTEE. Prepare the annual budget, review the budget execution, recommendations for investing of Club funds, coordination with other committees on budgetary concerns for submission to the Board of Directors, planning and programming for the financing of capital improvements and or alterations in conjunction with the planning committee.
      3. PLA ING COMMITTEE. Development of studies and plans involving capital improvements and alterations, coordination of projects with the Finance and Budget Committee and other committees and recommend project priorities to the Board of Directors, monitor the planning of a newsletter.
      4. ENTERTAINMENT COMMITTEE. Be responsible for the development and execution of the social activities progran1s in coordination with the House Committee.
      5. MEMBESHIP COMMITTEE. Maintain a list of all Club memberships, contact and recruit prospective members and stimulate their interest in a Club membership, interview, and make necessary investigations of all applicants and weighing membership requirements make recommendations to the Board of Directors.
      6. BYLAWS AND ARTICLES COMMITTEE. Study existing Bylaws and Articles and make proposals thereto, and recommend to the Board of Directors, such amendments it considers to be in the best interest of the Club.
      7. MAITEN AN CE COMMITTEE. Monitor the overall conditions of the Clubhouse, pool, te1mis courts, furniture and fixtures and all other prope1iies belonging to the Club thereto, evaluate the need for any repairs or replacements and take appropriate corrective action by submitting recommendations to the Board of Directors.
    3. No committee shall incur financial obligations exceeding the funds appropriated by the Board of Directors and approved by the membership until such proposed obligations are submitted and authorized by the Board of Directors.


    1. LIMITATIONS OF LIABILITY OF THE CLUB. The Club, its officers, Board of Directors and committee personnel shall not be liable or responsible for the destruction or loss of or damage or injury to the property of any member or of any guest of a member or visitor or other person using or enjoying the privileges of the Club, buildings or grounds, whether such property consists of cl~thing, sports equipment, or other articles about the Club grounds or buildings, nor shall the Club be liable or responsible for personal injury sustained by a member or guest. Any dispute shall be submitted to arbitration.
    2. RENUMERATION OF OFFICERS. No officer or director, committee member or member of any class of the Club shall receive any compensation for serving as such.
    3. INSURANCE. The Club shall maintain adequate fire and liability insurance on the buildings and facilities to protect the Club and its officers, Board of Directors and committee personnel from recourse.


    1. These Bylaws may be amended, repealed, and new Bylaws may be adopted at any regular or special meeting called for such purpose by the affirmative vote of a majority of the members, in person, or by proxy where a quorum is present.
    2.
